Childhood and Education

Einstein’s parents were secular, middle-class Jews. His father, Hermann Einstein, was originally a featherbed salesman and later ran an electrochemical factory with moderate success. His mother, the former Pauline Koch, ran the family household. He had one sister, Maria (who went by the name Maja), born two years after Albert. Einstein would write that two “wonders” deeply affected his early years. The first was his encounter with a compass at age five. He was mystified that invisible forces could deflect the needle. This would lead to a lifelong fascination with invisible forces. The second wonder came at age 12 when he discovered a book of geometry, which he devoured, calling it his “sacred little geometry book.”

Einstein's theory also presented the concept of time dilation, suggesting that time passes more slowly for objects in motion relative to those at rest. The faster an object moves, the more its clock slows down compared to a stationary observer. Additionally, the theory introduced the idea of length contraction, where objects in motion appear shorter along the direction of motion.

  • Family and Education : Einstein's family moved to Munich when he was a young child. His father, Hermann Einstein, and his mother, Pauline Einstein, ran an electrochemical factory. His family was of Jewish descent. Einstein attended a Catholic elementary school in Munich, where his unconventional nature sometimes clashed with the more rigid educational system.

Speech Difficulties: Einstein had speech difficulties as a child, and there were concerns that he might have learning disabilities. He often took his time to form sentences, and his teachers initially thought he was slow. However, his brilliance became evident as he excelled in subjects that captured his interest. Move to Italy: When Einstein was about 15, his family moved to Italy due to financial difficulties. He remained in Munich to complete his education but soon left school in frustration. He moved to Italy to join his family and continued his education there. University Education: Einstein eventually gained admission to the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH) in Zurich. He studied physics and mathematics and graduated in 1900.
